About this Qualification

The Pearson BTEC Level 1 Introductory in Public Services develops transferable and sector-specific skills. It is suitable for learners who want an introduction to the public services sector, and provides clear progression to Level 2 BTECs, apprenticeships and further training.

Assessment

All units are internally assessed and internally verified. There are no exams. Each unit is graded:

Pass Merit Distinction

Learners must complete summative assessment only after all teaching content has been covered.

Unit PS6 · 40 GLH · Sector
Taking Part in Fitness Testing
Plan, participate in, record and review fitness testing for public services entry
▶
Unit in brief: Learners develop skills in planning, taking part in, recording and reviewing fitness testing activities required for the public services. Covers basic fitness tests used in public services selection and reviewing performance against those standards.

Learning Aims: A — Plan and take part in fitness testing activities required for different public services  |  B — Record and review participation in fitness testing activities.

Unit PS9 · 40 GLH · Sector
Planning and Navigating a Route
OS map skills, route planning, navigation — practical field unit
▶
Unit in brief: Learners develop skills in planning and navigating a route using Ordnance Survey (OS) maps. They produce a route-planning card, consider risks and equipment, then navigate their planned route within an agreed timeframe.

Learning Aims: A — Plan a route using Ordnance Survey maps  |  B — Navigate an agreed route within an agreed timeframe.

Unit PS12 · 40 GLH · Sector
Taking Part in Sport and Adventurous Activities
Sport, teamwork and adventurous activities in a public services context
▶
Unit in brief: Learners develop participation skills in sport and adventurous activities, focusing on how these relate to working in a public service. Covers planning, health & fitness, communication and teamwork in individual sports, team sports and adventurous activities.
